Coffee

  • Ground Lewes Café
    Local favourite on Lansdown Road (turn right out of station past the Depot Cinema). The cappuccino is particularly good. Views of the South Downs while you wait.
  • Pestle and Mortar Café · Noodle bar · Asian grocery
    Noodle bar, café, tea room and Asian grocery rolled into one, with a lovely hidden garden out the back. Close to Ground Lewes.
  • Cafe Du Jardin French café
    Tucked in the courtyard of Pastorale Antiques on Malling Street. Open for French-style breakfasts (coffee, croissants, omelettes) and lunch (baked camembert, croque monsieur, steak frites). Hidden gem.

Food & Drink

  • The Swan Inn Pub · Lunch
    Beloved local pub at the end of Southover Street. Cosy with a roaring fire in winter and a chocolate-box cottage garden for summer. Simple, well-priced food — mackerel salad, Sunday roasts. Relaxed, time-worn atmosphere.
  • Anne of Cleves House Café Café · Garden
    Café inside the Tudor garden of the 16th-century Anne of Cleves House on Southover Street. A peaceful spot.

Shopping

  • Fifteenth Century Bookshop Bookshop
    A highlight of the High Street — rare and collectable books in a stunning yellow half-timbered building. At the foot of Keere Street, the town's most famous twitten.
  • The Needlemakers Shopping · Vintage
    Victorian red-brick landmark that was a WW1 needle factory. Now home to unusual boutiques across three floors. Don't miss the vintage shop downstairs with an incredible collection of vintage tins.
  • Lewes Flea Market Market · Antiques
    On Market Street, almost opposite The Needlemakers. All kinds of trinkets and curios — furniture, ceramics, jewellery, artwork.
  • Pastorale Antiques Antiques
    Malling Street, in a dreamy faded yellow building that was once a coaching inn. Specialises in French antiques; stock spills out into the courtyard. Also home to Cafe Du Jardin.
  • 71 The Maker's Gallery Jewellery · Gallery
    Beautiful jewellery and craft on the High Street.
  • Goods Lewes Homewares · Vintage
    Dreamy mix of vintage cane furniture and accessories like funky blankets. High Street.
  • From Victoria Gift shop
    Botanic-themed gift shop on the High Street.

Sights & Hidden Gems

  • Keere Street & the Twittens Walking · History
    Keere Street is Lewes's most famous twitten — a cobbled lane on an incline lined with heritage homes and secret gardens. The wider network of narrow Anglo-Saxon alleyways (Paine's Passage, St Swithuns Lane, Pipe Passage, Green Lane) is full of historic houses, trailing plants and great views.
  • Anne of Cleves House History · Museum
    16th-century timber-fronted Wealden hall house on Southover Street. Given to Anne of Cleves in her divorce settlement from Henry VIII in 1541 (she never lived here). Good period architecture and a Tudor garden.
  • The Round House Curiosity
    A bizarre home set inside an old windmill on Pipe Passage — bought by Virginia Woolf but never lived in. Easy to miss, worth finding.
  • Harvey's Brewery Landmark
    Pass it on Cliffe High Street heading east across the bridge over the River Ouse. Lewes's own brewery, a local institution.
  • Lewes Land Nature Reserve Nature · Green space
    A hidden town green space on an old railway yard reclaimed by nature, complete with a spiralling reed bed. Near the golf course with views across town.
  • Depot Cinema Cinema
    Independent arthouse cinema just outside the station on Lewes Road. Screens off-beat and independent films.
  • Glynde & Glyndebourne Opera House Day trip · Culture
    A footpath near Lewes heads to the pretty village of Glynde, home to the world-famous Glyndebourne Opera House. Worth the extra walk if you have time.
