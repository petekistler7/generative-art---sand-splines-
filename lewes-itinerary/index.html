Coffee

  • Ground Lewes Café

    The local favourite. Turn right out of the station past the Depot Cinema and right again onto Lansdown Road. The cappuccino is particularly good, and you can ponder views of the South Downs while you wait.

  • Pestle and Mortar Café · Noodle bar

    Noodle bar, café, tea room and Asian grocery rolled into one. There's a lovely hidden garden out the back. Close to Ground Lewes — worth knowing about for a longer lunch stop too.

  • Cafe Du Jardin French café

    A real hidden gem tucked in the courtyard of Pastorale Antiques on Malling Street. French-style breakfasts (coffee, croissants, omelettes) and proper lunches — baked camembert, croque monsieur, steak frites.

Food & Drink

  • The Swan Inn Pub · Lunch

    A beloved local at the end of Southover Street. Cosy with a roaring fire in winter; a chocolate-box cottage garden in summer. Simple, well-priced food — mackerel salad, Sunday roasts. Time-worn and genuinely local.

  • Anne of Cleves House Café Café · Garden

    Café inside the Tudor garden of the 16th-century Anne of Cleves House on Southover Street. A calm, historic spot for a mid-afternoon break.

Shopping

  • Fifteenth Century Bookshop Bookshop

    One of the highlights of the High Street — rare and collectable books in a stunning yellow half-timbered building. Sits at the foot of Keere Street, the town's most famous twitten.

  • The Needlemakers Shopping · Vintage

    A Victorian red-brick landmark, once a WW1 needle factory, now full of unusual independent boutiques across three floors. The vintage shop downstairs has an incredible collection of old tins.

  • Lewes Flea Market Market · Antiques

    On Market Street, almost opposite The Needlemakers. A good rummage spot — furniture, ceramics, jewellery, artwork and all kinds of curios.

  • Pastorale Antiques Antiques

    A dreamy faded yellow building on Malling Street that was once a coaching inn. French antique specialists — stock spills across the courtyard and up the stairs. Also home to Cafe Du Jardin.

  • 71 The Maker's Gallery Jewellery · Gallery

    Beautiful independent jewellery and craft on the High Street. Hard to walk past without stopping.

  • Goods Lewes Homewares · Vintage

    A dreamy mix of vintage cane furniture and accessories — funky blankets, cushions, home objects. High Street.

  • From Victoria Gift shop

    Botanic-themed gift shop on the High Street. Good for presents.

Sights & Hidden Gems

  • Keere Street & the Twittens Walking · History

    Keere Street is the most famous twitten in Lewes — a cobbled lane on an incline lined with heritage homes and secret gardens. The wider network of Anglo-Saxon alleyways (Paine's Passage, St Swithuns Lane, Pipe Passage, Green Lane) is full of historic houses, trailing plants and unexpected views. Don't rush through them.

  • Anne of Cleves House History · Museum

    A 16th-century timber-fronted Wealden hall house on Southover Street, given to Anne of Cleves as part of her divorce settlement from Henry VIII in 1541 — though she never actually lived here. Lovely period architecture and a Tudor garden.

  • The Round House Curiosity

    A peculiar home set inside an old windmill on Pipe Passage — bought by Virginia Woolf, but never lived in. Easy to walk past without noticing. Worth seeking out.

  • Harvey's Brewery Landmark

    Pass it heading east along Cliffe High Street toward the river. Lewes's own brewery and a proper local institution — you'll likely smell it before you see it.

  • Lewes Land Nature Reserve Nature · Green space

    A hidden town green space on an old railway yard, quietly reclaimed by nature. Complete with a spiralling reed bed. Near the golf course, with views back across the town.

  • Depot Cinema Cinema

    An independent arthouse cinema right outside the station on Lewes Road. Shows off-beat and independent films — a good option if the weather turns.

  • Glynde & Glyndebourne Opera House Day trip · Culture

    A footpath from near Lewes leads to the pretty village of Glynde and the world-famous Glyndebourne Opera House. Worth the extra mile if you have time to spare.
